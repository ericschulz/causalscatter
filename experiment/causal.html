Instructions:

Please read the following instructions very carefully:


In the following study, it is your task to distinguish cause from effect.

You will see two different plots of the same data. It is your task to decide which of the two plots is more likely to be true. Truth here means that the labels cause and effect are on the correct axis. Some examples of cause and effect are: altitude causes lower temperature, heat causes water to boil, the frequency of brushing one's teeths causes different numbers of cavities, etc.

You have a total number of 80 trials ahead of you. That means you have 80 chances to indicate which of the two plots you think is more likely to have the cause and the effect-label on the correct axis.

The data you will see are actual data sets, where one variable has caused the other. We are interested in seeing whether you can tell which one is which and indicate that by selecting which of the two variables is more likely to be the cause and therefore on the horizontal x-axis and which one the effect and therefore should be on the vertical y-axis.

Afterwards, you will be rewarded based on the true underlying relations of cause and effect and how well you were able to determine those.

For every task, you will see a page with the instructions, an input slider, and two plots of the same data, but with exchanged axes accordingly. You can hide the instructions by clicking "Hide explanations". You can show them by clicking "Show explanations".



Please click on the button below to start the task.

Explanation:

III. Remember, it is your task to indicate which of the two plots is more likely, that is which of the two variables is more likely to have caused the other variable and therefore should be put on the x-axis.

IV. On top of the graph, you can see how many invesitgations you have left.

V. Please use the slider to indicate how likely you think the plots are (-100=it is definitely the left plot; 100=it is definitely the right plot; 0=both are equally likely).
